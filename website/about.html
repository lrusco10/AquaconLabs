The Aquatic Conservation Lab

(Also known as AQUACON Lab)

Our Mission

The Aquatic Conservation Lab (AQUACON Lab) is dedicated to safeguarding the health and resilience of aquatic ecosystems across diverse geographies, from the Great Lakes to the Atlantic and Pacific Northwest waterways. We tackle critical 21st-century challenges facing aquatic life by integrating advanced scientific methodologies with practical, applied solutions. Our interdisciplinary team leverages genomics and bioinformatics to understand population dynamics and genetic health, employs ecological field techniques (including exploring the use of autonomous underwater vehicles for habitat monitoring), and increasingly incorporates artificial intelligence and machine learning for automated classification and data analysis. We are committed to fostering innovative conservation and management strategies for a wide array of aquatic organisms, including fish, crustaceans, mussels, and plants, in close collaboration with state and federal fisheries biologists, as well as non-governmental organizations (NGOs).
