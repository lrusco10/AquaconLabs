Our Research

S.O.A.C is dedicated to offering students opportunities to engage in meaningful research projects that enhance their academic and professional growth.

This multi-year study explores how urban pollinator gardens affect biodiversity, air quality, and community engagement. Our findings so far indicate increases in native pollinator species and local use of green spaces.

The project also partners with local schools to provide educational opportunities about pollination and sustainable gardening.

This project involves training volunteers to collect water samples across various points in the river system. The data helps identify pollution sources and informs policy recommendations for local governments.

The project is a collaboration between our lab, local schools, and environmental agencies.
